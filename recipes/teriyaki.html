Teriyaki Chicken

Teriyaki chicken is a savory-sweet dish made by cooking tender chicken in a
glossy sauce of soy sauce, sugar, and ginger. Itâ€™s often served with rice
and vegetables for a flavorful, satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 1 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1/4 cup mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
  • 4 tsp grated fresh ginger
  • 3 gloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400F (or 200C)
  2. Whisk cornstarch and water in a small bowl until dissolved.
  3. Combine soy sauce, sugar, mirin, ginger, garlic, and
    red pepper flakes in a saucepan over medium heat to simmer.
  4. Slowly whisk in cornstarch-water mixture and bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer until thickened,
    stirring often, for 10 minutes.
  6. Pat chicken dry with paper towels; arrange in baking dish
    and pour sauce evenly over chicken breasts.
  7. Bake until no more pink in center, and juices run clear (25-30 minutes).
    An instant-read-thermometer inserted into the center should read atleast 165F.
